## **Daily Sentences / Phrases**

### **Day 1: Привіт!**

**Translation:** “Hello!”

**Breakdown:**

- Привіт — Hello

**Usage:** Informal greeting used with friends, family, or people your age.\
**Formal alternatives:** Добрий день! / Добрий ранок! / Добрий вечір!

### **Day 2: Як тебе звати?**

**Translation:** “What is your name?”\
**Literal Translation:** “How (do they) call you?”\
**Formal Version:** Як вас звати?

**Breakdown:**

- Як — how
- тебе — you (accusative)
- звати — to call

**Grammar Note:**\
Impersonal use of **звaти** (“to call by name”) — no explicit subject.

### **Day 3: Мене звати Джош.**

**Translation:** “My name is Josh.”\
**Literal Translation:** “They call me Josh.”

**Breakdown:**

- Мене — me (accusative)
- звати — to call
- Джош — Josh

**Grammar Note:**\
Common structure for introductions — **Мене звати [name]**, **Тебе звати [name]**, etc.\
No need for “to be” (бути) in the present tense; it’s implied.

### **Day 4: Радий тебе зустріти.**

**Translation:** “Nice to meet you.”\
**Literal Translation:** “Glad to meet you.”\
**Feminine Form:** Рада тебе зустріти.

**Breakdown:**

- Радий — glad
- тебе — you (accusative)
- зустріти — to meet

**Grammar Note:**\
Adjective **радий/рада** agrees with the speaker’s gender.\
The verb **зустріти** is perfective, meaning the meeting is a completed action.

### **Day 5: Як ти?**

**Translation:** “How are you?”\
**Literal Translation:** “How you?”\
**Formal Version:** Як ви?

**Breakdown:**

- Як — how
- ти — you

**Grammar Note:**\
The verb “to be” (**є**) is dropped in the present tense, though implied: _Як ти (є)?_ → _Як ти?_

### **Day 6: Добрий ранок!**

**Translation:** “Good morning!”\
**Literal Translation:** “Good morning.”

**Breakdown:**

- Добрий — good
- ранок — morning

**Grammar Note:**\
Adjective **добрий** agrees in gender with **ранок** (masculine).\
For “Good night,” use **Добра ніч** (feminine form).

### **Day 7: Гарного дня!**

**Translation:** “Have a good day!”\
**Literal Translation:** “Of a good day.”

**Breakdown:**

- Гарного — good (genitive masculine)
- дня — day (genitive)

**Grammar Note:**\
Uses **genitive case** to express a wish or blessing.\
The adjective **гарного** agrees in case, number, and gender with **дня**.
